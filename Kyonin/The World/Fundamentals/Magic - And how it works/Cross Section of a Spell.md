### This is how magic is broken down from the perspective of wizards within the world of [[Kyonin]]:

# What makes up a given spell?
Spells are broken up into several "pieces". Each of these pieces is **important**, and *necessary*\* for a spell to "succeed". - (Meaning it'll have the desired affect once the casting is complete)
- The fundamental's of these "pieces" acted as the blueprint for [[firearms]] when they were invented.

tab: #### 1. [[Sigils]] - The Body
Sigil is a general use term for a drawing, carving, etching, etc. used to evoke some form of spell craft.
- **e.g.**, A transmutation circle. Protective wards. Nercromantic seal.
---

The most important piece to any good *sigil* is the substance used to create it's many parts. With that being said however, there isn't one singular "**best**" option. Most of the time a spellcaster will use whatever is available to them, and then adjust the remaining pieces of the spell to achieve their desired affect. Throughout the planes of existence there are *thousands* of different medium's for the creation of sigils, but millage may vary.
- **e.g.**, You may want to cast a spell that uses a type of wood and a being's blood as material components. You could try any of the following and achieve your desired affect.
	- Carve the sigil into a tree and then paint over the sigil with the blood of the given being to meet both the structural requirements of the spell as well as the material components.
	- Paint the sigil on the ground with the being's blood and simply supply the wood as the material required, either in the sigil or held on your person.
	- Write the sigil out in ink within a spell book on your person, and then supply the the materials required.

tab: ##### [[Runes]]
To start, first, we must address that "Runes" is a colloquial term for any kind of message, whether coded or not, written within a sigil. 
The astute among you may ask: "Won't that mess up a spells affect? Why would you want to write messages within a sigil?". Put simply, no it won't ruin a spell, but there are a few caveats.
- If you plan to use the same substance to create the main lines and shapes of the sigil and whatever runic message you're looking to write out, you **MUST** make sure that the runic message does **NOT** touch the rest of the sigil. This can and will destroy, or reshape, a spell.
- If you're preforming any kind of ritual spell with a "consumption circle" make sure that the symbol that triggers the consumption is the only marking within the circle. 

But why add a rune to begin with? What's the point? Well adding runes to a sigil is an ancient tradition going back to the origins of spell craft. In the early days of spell craft those first casters needed a way to communicate to other casters what they'd discovered about one of the aspects of a spell without the general public, or rival casters, learning dangerous secrets of spell craft. So they created and used a runic language devised by different factions of spellcasters.

Today runes are mainly used by the novice spellcaster, to learn what to do and not to do with a given spell, and master spellcasters for coding and hiding the intent of their spells.

tab: ##### [[Symbols]]
Symbols is another general use term designed to be a "catch-all" term for any of the alchemical, religious, and demonic symbols used within the more intricate parts of a sigil, like a consumption circle, *i.e.*, a designated spot for material components to be consumed by a spell by envoking some element or higher power.
- **e.g.**, Putting the symbols for projection, `♓︎`, and, multiplication, `♒︎`, is one of the core components of the spell scorching ray. But by adding a blank comsumption circle with the symbol for air, `🜁`, surrounded by fire, `🜂`, and multiplication around the main spell you'll cast it at a "higher level" and multiply the number of rays, but also increase the amount air needed to cast.
